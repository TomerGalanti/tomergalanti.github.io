About

Howdy! We are the Deep Learning Fundamentals group at Texas A&M, led by Prof. Tomer Galanti. We combine theory and experiments to understand representation learning dynamics (e.g., collapse and alignment), generalization and compression in modern architectures, and practical algorithms for training and serving LLMs at scale.

We welcome collaborations and visitors. Get in touch or see open positions.

Research Areas

Representation structure & dynamics

Ranks, collapse, alignment, and their links to generalization and transfer across layers and modalities.

Neural collapse Alignment Theory

Generalization, compression & adaptation

How training hyperparameters and architectural priors (e.g., convolutional sparsity) shape compressibility and few-shot learning.

Compression Few-shot Transfer

Efficient training & inference for LLMs

Parallel speculative decoding, structured language models, and fairness/robustness trade-offs in token distributions.
